Consider an elemental ring of thickness \(\,dx\) at a distance \(x\) from the center of mass of a hollow cylinder [FigureÂ 7.1.10.(a)], then the moment of inertia of this ring is given by
\begin{equation*} \,dI=\,dm\,R^{2} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad I = \int\limits_{0}^{l}\left(\frac{M}{2\pi Rl}\right)(2\pi R \,dx)R^{2}=\frac{MR^{2}}{l}\left(l\right)_{0}^{l}=MR^{2} \end{equation*}
[Mass of an elemental ring = (mass per unit area of a cylinder) (area of a ring).] Here \(M\) is mass of the cylinder, \(R\) is its radius, and \(l\) is its length.
