Johannes Kepler established three laws for planetary motion from his years of observation in \(16^{th}\) century [Figure FigureÂ 6.2.1.(a)]. They are
  1. The planets move along elliptical paths, and the sun is located at one of the focii.
  2. The planet sweeps out equal areas in equal interval of time
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\,dA_{1}}{\,dt} = \frac{\,dA_{2}}{\,dt} \end{equation*}
  3. The square of the orbital period of a planet is directly proportional to the cube of the semimajor axis of its orbit
    \begin{equation*} T^{2} \propto a^{3}. \end{equation*}
