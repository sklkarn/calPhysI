Solution 3.4.5.1.

  1. \begin{align*} v_{ox} \amp = vo \cos\theta_{o};\qquad \theta_{o} = 60^{o}; \qquad v_{oy} = vo \sin\theta_{o};\\ x \amp = 40 yd = 120 ft; \quad y_i=0,\quad y_f=0\\ \text{But,} \quad x \amp = v_{ox} t; \qquad \text{and}\quad g = 32 ft/s^{2} \\ y_f-y_i \amp = v_{oy} t - \frac{1}{2} g t^{2};\\ 0 \amp= vo \sin\theta_{o}\left( \frac{x}{vo \cos\theta_{o}}\right)- \frac{1}{2}\times 32 \left(\frac{x}{vo \cos\theta_{o}}\right)^{2}\\ \therefore \quad v_{o} \amp = 62.3 ft/s \end{align*}
  2. \(\displaystyle t = 3.85 s\)
  3. \begin{align*} v_{y}\amp = v_{oy} - g t;\\ v_{y} \amp = 0 \\ t \amp = 1.68 s; \\ y \amp = v_{oy} t - \frac{1}{2}g t^{2} = 45.48 ft \end{align*}
  4. \begin{align*} x \amp = v t; \quad t = 3.85 s\\ x = 60 yd - 40 yd = 20 yd = 60 ft \amp = v t \\ \therefore v \amp = \frac{60}{3.85} =15.58 ft/s \end{align*}
