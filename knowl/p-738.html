A planet moving around a circular path needs a centripetal force which is provided by the force of attraction between the planet and the sun.
\begin{equation*} F=\frac{GMm}{r^{2}}=\frac{mv^{2}}{r} \end{equation*}
where \(M,\quad m,\) and \(r\) are the mass of the sun, mass of the planet, and radius of a circular path, respectively.
\begin{equation} \therefore\quad v=\sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}} \tag{6.2.1} \end{equation}
But, the velocity of the planet in the orbit is given by
\begin{equation} v=\frac{2\pi r}{T} \tag{6.2.2} \end{equation}
where \(T\) is the time period of revolution of the planet around the sun.
\begin{equation*} \therefore\quad \frac{4\pi^{2} r^{2}}{T^{2}} = \frac{GM}{r} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad T^{2} = r^{3}\left(\frac{4\pi^{2}}{GM}\right) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad T^{2}\propto r^{3} \end{equation*}
