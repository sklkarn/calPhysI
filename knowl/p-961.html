Specific Gravity (SG): It is the ratio of density of a fluid to the density of water at \(4^{o}C\text{.}\) It is therefore a dimensionless quantity.
\begin{equation*} SG = \frac{\rho_{f}}{\rho_{o}} \end{equation*}
Hence, SG of a fluid has the same numerical value as its density in any systems of unit. Since density of water at \(4^{o}C\) is \(1 \,g/ml \) or \(1000 \,kg/m^{3}\) water is normally used as a reference material when calculating the specific gravity for any other materials atleast in CGS and SI system of units. For example, specific gravity of iron is 7.85 because the density of iron is \(7850 \,kg/m^{3}\) and density of water at \(4^{o}C\) is \(1000 kg/m^{3}\text{.}\)Hence
\begin{equation*} SG(iron) = \frac{7850 [kg/m^{3}]}{1000 [kg/m^{3}] } = 7.85. \end{equation*}
