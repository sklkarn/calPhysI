Velocity of a person with respect to ground, \(v_{pg}=3 m/s\text{,}\) velocity of rain with respect to person, \(v_{rp} = 4m/s,\) and velocity of rain with respect to ground, \(v_{rg}\text{.}\) Hence,
\begin{align} \vec{v}_{rg} \amp = a\hat{i}+ b\hat{j}\tag{3.2.6}\\ \vec{v}_{pg} \amp = 3\hat{i} \tag{3.2.7}\\ \therefore \vec{v}_{rp}\amp = 4\hat{j} = \vec{v}_{rg}-\vec{v}_{pg} = a\hat{i}+ b\hat{j} - 3\hat{i} = (a-3)\hat{i}+ b\hat{j} \tag{3.2.8} \end{align}
Equating on both side of the equation, we get - \(a=3 m/s,\) and \(b= 4m/s\)
\begin{equation} \therefore \vec{v}_{rg} = 3\hat{i}+ 4\hat{j} \tag{3.2.9} \end{equation}
Now if rain makes an angle \(\theta\) with the vertical, then
\begin{equation*} \theta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{3}{4}\right) = 37^{o} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} v_{rg} =\sqrt{3^{2}+4^{2}} = 5 \end{equation*}
