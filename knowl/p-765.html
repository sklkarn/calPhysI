\begin{equation*} I=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n} m_{i}r^{2}_{i} = m_{1} r^{2}_{1} + m_{2} r_{2}^{2}+\cdots , \end{equation*}
as shown in figure Figure 7.1.1.(b). An extended object consists of a continuous distribution of mass at a continually varying distance from the axis of rotation, its moment of inertia can be written as,
\begin{equation*} I=\int\,dI=\int\limits_{0}^{M} r^{2}\,dm \end{equation*}
where \(\,dm\) is a differential (infinitely small) mass of the object [figure Figure 7.1.1.(c)] and \(r\) is a distance of this differential mass from axis of rotation.
