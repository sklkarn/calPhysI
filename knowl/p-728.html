Acceleration due to gravity on the surface of the earth is given by
\begin{equation*} g=\frac{GM}{R^{2}} \end{equation*}
At height \(h\) from the surface of the earth [Figure FigureÂ 6.1.4], acceleration due to gravity is given by
\begin{equation*} g_{h} = \frac{GM}{(R+h)^{2}} = \frac{GM}{R^{2}\left(1+\frac{h}{R}\right)^{2}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\frac{g}{\left(1+\frac{h}{R}\right)^{2}}=g\left(1+\frac{h}{R}\right)^{-2} =g\left(1-\frac{2h}{R} +\cdots\right) \end{equation*}
using binomial theorem
\begin{equation*} \therefore \quad g_{h} =g\left(1-\frac{2h}{R}\right) \end{equation*}
