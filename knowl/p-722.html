Since the centripetal force \(\vec{F}_{c}=m\vec{a}_{c}\) required by the mass m to follow a circle of radius r, then this force must be provided by the vector difference in force of gravity \(\vec{F_{e}}=m\vec{g}_{e} \) and a real force \(F=m\vec{g}\text{,}\) i.e.,
\begin{equation*} \vec{F}_{c}=\vec{F_{e}}-\vec{F} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad m\vec{a}_{c} = m\vec{g}_{e}-m\vec{g} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore \vec{g}=\vec{g}_{e}-\vec{a}_{c} \end{equation*}
The magnitude of \(\vec{g}\) can be found by
\begin{equation*} \vec{g}\cdot\vec{g} =g^{2}= \left(\vec{g}_{e}-\vec{a}_{c}\right) \cdot\left(\vec{g}_{e}-\vec{a}_{c}\right) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \vec{g}_{e}\cdot\vec{g}_{e} + \vec{a}_{c}\cdot\vec{a}_{c}-2\vec{g}_{e}\cdot\vec{a}_{c} =g^{2}_{e}+a^{2}_{c}-2g_{e}a_{c}\cos\lambda \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore g= \sqrt{g^{2}_{e}+a^{2}_{c}-2g_{e}a_{c}\cos\lambda} \end{equation*}
where
\begin{equation*} a_{c}=r\omega^{2} = R\omega^{2}\cos\lambda, \end{equation*}
R is the radius of the earth, and the acceleration due to gravity on a non-rotating earth is
\begin{equation*} g_{e}= \frac{GM}{R^{2}}. \end{equation*}
