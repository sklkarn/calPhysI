An aluminum weight of mass 200 g is attached by a short string to the bottom of a float of density \(0.3 g/cm^{3}\) which is supported by another string attached to a mass balance. When both the weight and the float are completely immersed in water the balance reads 50 g. What is
  1. the volume of the float,
  2. the mass of the float,
  3. the buoyant force of the water on the float, and
  4. the buoyant force of the water on the aluminum weight.
  5. What would the balance read if only the weight were under water?
