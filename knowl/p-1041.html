The natural tendency of a liquid is to contract its surface area to minimum its size. Therefore, work is required to increase the surface area of a liquid film. Increasing the surface area increasing the energy of the film. Hence, surface tension is the energy (or work) required to increase the surface area of a liquid by unit.
