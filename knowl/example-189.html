Example 10.7.4.

A spherical balloon one foot in diameter contains helium at one atmosphere of pressure and at a temperature of \(27\,^{o}C\text{.}\) Find
  1. the number of moles of helium in the balloon,
  2. the number of molecules in the balloon,
  3. the average kinetic energy of each helium molecule,
  4. the rms velocity of a helium molecule,
  5. the amount of heat (in Joules) to increase the temperature of the helium in the balloon by one degree Celsius if the balloon is made of rubber that allows the pressure to remain at one atmosphere,
  6. the amount of heat (in Joules) to increase the temperature of the helium in the balloon by one degree Celsius if the balloon is made of non-stretching aluminum so the volume remains constant,
  7. the final temperature of the balloon if it rises adiabatically to an altitude where the pressure is one-half atmosphere.
