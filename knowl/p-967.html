\begin{equation*} p = \frac{F}{A}, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} p_{o} = 1 \,atm = 1.013\times 10^{5} \,Pa = 1.013 \,bar = 14.7 \,lb/in^{2}=14.7 \,psi. \end{equation*}
Here \(p_{o}\) is an atmospheric pressure. The unit of pressure is Pascal (Pa) in SI system.
\begin{equation*} 1 \,Pa=1 \,N/m^{2}, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 1 \,bar = 0.1 \,MPa, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 1 \,torr = 1 \,mm Hg = \frac{1}{0.76} \,atm. \end{equation*}
1 atm = 760 mm of Hg (mercury) at STP (standard temperature and pressure i.e., T=273.15 K and p = 1 atm). Pressure is also measured in psi (pound of force per square inch).
