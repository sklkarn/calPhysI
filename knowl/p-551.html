Since lever works at rotational equilibrium, the total torque must be zero, i.e.,
\begin{equation*} \sum\Gamma =0. \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} \therefore F_{in}\times e = F_{out}\times l \tag{5.2.2} \end{equation}
Where \(e\) and \(l\) are lengths of effort arm and load arm, respectively. From eqns. (5.2.1) and (5.2.2), we have -
\begin{equation*} \frac{F_{out}}{F_{in}} = \frac{d_{in}}{d_{out}} = \frac{e}{l} \end{equation*}
