Moment of inertia is a property of a material during rotation. It is a measure of sluggishness (resistance to any changes) in object’s rotational motion. Moment of inertia depends on the shape, mass distribution, and axis of rotation of the object. It is a rotational analog of a mass for linear motion. For a point mass the moment of inertia is just the product of mass and the square of perpendicular distance to the rotation axis. If a point object has a mass \(m\) and is rotating about an axis then its moment of inertia, \(I\) is given by \(I=mr^{2}.\) where \(r\) is a distance of the object from the axis of rotation [figure Figure 7.1.1.(a)].
