Item ii.

If the body is floating in the fluid [FigureÂ 9.1.4.(d)], then buoyant force is greater than the weight of the body. Hence body comes to a float, but at the surface of the fluid, net force acting on the body is zero.
\begin{equation*} B=m_{b}g \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad V_{df}\rho_{f} g = V_{b}\rho_{b} g \end{equation*}
Since body is floating in the fluid, volume of the displaced fluid is then only equal to immersed portion of the body. Hence \(V_{df} \lt V_{b}.\)
\begin{equation*} \text{or,}\quad \frac{\rho_{f}}{\rho_{b}} = \frac{V_{b}}{V_{df}} \gt 1 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \therefore \quad \rho_{f} \gt \rho_{b} \end{equation*}
The body will float in a fluid if the densities of fluid is greater than that of the body.
