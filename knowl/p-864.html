A uniform cylinder of mass 100 kg has a radius of 30 cm and spins freely about its axis of symmetry. One end of a string is attached to the surface of the cylinder so that the string winds and unwinds as the cylinder rotates first one direction and then the other. A constant tension of 100 N is maintained in the string by a constant vector force applied to the other end of the string. At time t = 0, the string is completely unwound and the cylinder is spinning at 30 rad/s.
  1. What is the moment of inertia of the cylinder?
  2. As long as \(\theta \gt \frac{\pi}{2},\) or \(\theta \lt -\frac{\pi}{2}\text{,}\) the torque on the cylinder is constant in magnitude. Why? What is this torque?
  3. Ignoring the first quarter revolution, how long does it take the cylinder to stop?
  4. What is the angular acceleration of the cylinder when it is slowing down?
  5. What is the minimum length of string that will stop the cylinder?
  6. What is the initial kinetic energy of the cylinder?
  7. What is the initial angular momentum of the cylinder?
  8. If the cylinder is rotating counter-clockwise, what is the direction of the angular momentum vector?
