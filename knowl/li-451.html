Gravitational Potential Energy: Suppose a body of mass \(m\) is lifted to a height \(h\) against gravity without acceleration, then work done by the applied force, \(F_{a}\) is given by
\begin{equation*} W_{a}= F_{a}\cdot h= -F_{g}\cdot h =-mgh \end{equation*}
This work is stored in the body in the form of gravitational potential energy. Hence when this body is released from the position the body drops down to the ground again. Therefore, the work done by the gravity to pull that object towards earthâ€™s surface is given by
\begin{equation*} W_{g}= F_{g}h = mgh = PE \end{equation*}
This is gravitational potential energy in a uniform gravitational field, \(g\text{.}\) The gravitational potential energy due to two point masses is given by
\begin{equation*} -G\frac{Mm}{r}. \end{equation*}
Where \(M\) is the mass of an object at the origin, \(m\) is the mass of an object at a radial distance \(r\) from the origin and the zero point is chosen at infinity.
