Problem Solving Technique: To determine the center of mass of a uniformly distributed body, one performs the following steps:
  1. Break the body down into its constituent parts.
  2. Specify the length, area, or volume of each constituent part.
  3. Locate the coordinates of the center of mass of each constituent part.
  4. Utilize the equations shown above using length, area, or volume in place of the mass.
    \begin{equation*} e.g.,\quad x_{cm} = \frac{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{A_{i}x_{i}}}{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{A_{i}}} \end{equation*}

  5. If an object has a uniform mass distribution (uniform density), then its geometric center is taken as center of mass.
