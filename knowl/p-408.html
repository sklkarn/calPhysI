The center of (mass) gravity (point \(G\)) of any body can be determined by suspending the body by a cord attached at point \(A\) and then by a cord attached at \(C\text{.}\) When the body is suspended from \(A\text{,}\) the line \(AD\) is vertical; when it is suspended from \(C\text{,}\) the line \(CE\) is vertical. The center of gravity is at the intersection of \(AD\) and \(CE\) [FigureÂ 4.10.1]. When an object is suspended from any single point, its center of gravity lies directly beneath that point. For a group of discrete masses the location of center of mass can be defined as:
