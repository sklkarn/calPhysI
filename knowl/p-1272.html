Paragraph

  1. for a monoatomic gas, f = 3 translational motion = 3 degrees of freedom.
    \begin{equation*} \therefore\quad C_{V} = \frac{f}{2}R = \frac{3}{2}R \end{equation*}
  2. for a diatomic gas, f = 3 translational + 2rotational motion = 5 degrees of freedom.
    \begin{equation*} \therefore\quad C_{V} = \frac{f}{2}R = \frac{5}{2}R \end{equation*}
  3. for a diatomic gas with vibration, f = 3 translational + 2rotational + 2 vibrational motions = 7 degrees of freedom. (in 2 vibrational motions we have: 1 dimensional stretch and 1 dimensional bend)
    \begin{equation*} \therefore\quad C_{V} = \frac{f}{2}R = \frac{7}{2}R \end{equation*}
  4. for solids, f = 3 D kinetic energy + 3 D potential energy = 6 degrees of freedom.
    \begin{equation*} \therefore\quad C_{V} = \frac{f}{2}R = \frac{6}{2}R =3R \end{equation*}
