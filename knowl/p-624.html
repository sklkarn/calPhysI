  1. For inelastic collision: If the two objects stick together and move off at an angle \(\theta_f\) to the \(x\)-axis with speed \(v_f\) [Figure 5.4.2.(a)]. Then from principle of conservation of momentum,
    \begin{equation} m_{1}u_{1} +m_{2}u_{2}\cos\theta_{i} = (m_{1}+m_{2})v_{f}\cos\theta_{f} \quad [\text{along x-axis}]\tag{5.4.3} \end{equation}
    \begin{equation} 0 +m_{2}u_{2}\sin\theta_{i} = (m_{1}+m_{2})v_{f}\sin\theta_{f} \quad [\text{along y-axis}]\tag{5.4.4} \end{equation}
  2. For elastic collision [Figure 5.4.2.(b)]:
    \begin{equation} m_{1}u_{1} +m_{2}u_{2}\cos\theta_{2i} = m_{1}v_{1}\cos\theta_{1f}+m_{2}v_{2}\cos\theta_{2f} \quad [\text{along x-axis}]\tag{5.4.5} \end{equation}
    \begin{equation} 0 + m_{2}u_{2}\sin\theta_{2i} = m_{1}v_{1}\sin\theta_{1f}-m_{2}v_{2}\sin\theta_{2f} \quad [\text{along y-axis}]\tag{5.4.6} \end{equation}
    \begin{equation} \frac{1}{2}m_{1}u_{1}^{2}+\frac{1}{2}m_{2}u_{2}^{2} =\frac{1}{2}m_{1}v_{1}^{2}+\frac{1}{2}m_{2}v_{2}^{2} \tag{5.4.7} \end{equation}
These equations can help sove the 2D collision problems.
