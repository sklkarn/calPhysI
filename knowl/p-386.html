\begin{equation} T\cos\theta=mg \tag{4.7.1} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} T\sin\theta=\frac{mv^{2}}{r} \tag{4.7.2} \end{equation}
Dividing eqn. (4.7.2) with (4.7.1), we get -
\begin{equation*} \tan\theta=\frac{v^{2}}{rg} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} or, \quad \theta=\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{v^{2}}{rg}\right) \end{equation*}
The time period \(T'\) can be given by
\begin{equation*} T'=\frac{2\pi r}{v}= 2\pi\frac{r}{\sqrt{rg\tan\theta}}=2\pi\sqrt{\frac{r}{g\tan\theta}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{l\sin\theta}{g\tan\theta}}=2\pi\sqrt{\frac{l\cos\theta}{g}} \end{equation*}
If \(\theta \to 90^{o}\text{,}\) then tension in the string \(T \to \infty\text{,}\) which is not possible. Hence the body never receives the same height as its fixed support.
